CycleHack Twin Cities is part of an international CycleHack event which originated in Glasgow, Scotland last summer. CycleHack focuses on bringing people and their places together to discuss and generate solutions to barriers that stop or inhibit people from cycling.

The Ideation ride will be on Friday, June 19, 7PM to 9PM. Join us for a pre-hack brainstorming bike ride! We will be taking a leisurely, relaxed-pace ride with various stops to discuss barriers to cycling and ways we might address them. The ride will be from 7PM to 9PM and will start at start at Recreation Center at Matthews Park in the Seward neighborhood (2318 29th Ave S, Minneapolis, 55406).

There are a number of bike-related datasets that might be useful in what you want to build. Here is a list of some of them:

  • Nice Ride data feed can be found on their map. Specifically secure.niceridemn.org/data2/stations.json.
  • The Bike Data Project looks to have some great data. They require an email to get access to the data so you should think about doing this before the Hack Day.
  • A collection of links to bike share datasets from Motivateco which runs a number of bike shares.
  • Minneapolis Pedestration and Bike trails.
  • Hennepin County open data portal (no specific bike datasets).
  • MetroGIS has some bike data on their Data finder site.
  • Possible bike data on the Minnesota Geospatial Commons data portal.
  • Open Twin Cities' list of data portals.
